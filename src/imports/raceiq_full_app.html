Traditional Data
Snapshots Only
Post position · Finish order · Lengths behind at 5 checkpoints · Surface · Distance · Purse. They only tell you where a horse was — not how it got there. Did it move from 4th to 1st in a burst or a slow grind? Did it waste ground? Where did it fatigue?
GPS Data
The Full Picture
Sectional time · Running time · Speed every 50m · Distance ran (path efficiency) · Stride count per gate · Time & distance behind the leader · Speed change finish vs. start. GPS fills every gap between those 5 checkpoints.
Proxy Mode
No GPS? Estimated Metrics
At non-GPS tracks: Early/Late Position replaces Speed · Late Pace Figure replaces Stride Efficiency · Extra Distance estimated from post position + track bias data. Same card, slightly wider confidence band. Cards show 📊 Proxy badge.
Why Stride Efficiency Matters
Winners hold their stride at the finish. Losing horses take more steps as they tire — same distance, less power per stride. That's fatigue made visible.

Race IQ™ — About This Tool
Race IQ uses Equibase GPS data to surface insights that finish positions cannot show. Win probabilities are weighted composites of late speed percentile (40%), stride efficiency percentile (35%), and position gain history (25%). Proxy mode estimates GPS metrics from traditional pace figures at non-GPS tracks.
⚠️ Known Limitations
GPS Coverage is Partial
→Only select tracks have GPS equipment. Cards show 📊 Proxy badge when data is estimated — confidence intervals are wider.
Small Sample Sizes
→Horses with only 1–2 GPS races produce less reliable win probabilities. More GPS starts = more accurate predictions.
Track Conditions Not Modeled
→GPS stride data from a fast track doesn't transfer directly to a sloppy/muddy condition. Weather is not yet factored in.
Win % is a Model, Not a Guarantee
→45% win probability means the model gives a 45% chance — it does not mean the horse will win 45% of the time going forward.
